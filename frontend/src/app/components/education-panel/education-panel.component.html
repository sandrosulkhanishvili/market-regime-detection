What is a Market Regime?

Financial markets don't behave the same way all the time. They switch between distinct regimes — persistent states with different risk/return profiles. Identifying the current regime helps investors adjust strategy: ride bull markets, hedge in crises, and stay patient in sideways periods.

How does K-Means find regimes?

Imagine plotting every trading day as a dot on a 2D chart: X = daily return, Y = volatility. Days with similar behavior cluster together visually. K-Means automates this: it places 3 "center points" (centroids) and assigns each dot to its nearest center — no human labeling required.
